The Museum Companion

Project Overview

The Museum Planning Companion is a digital tool designed to bridge the gap between educational goals and logistical reality. By providing a centralized planning space, it transforms the field trip experience for educators, allowing them to focus on learning rather than logistics.

The Problem

Tara, an elementary school teacher managing groups of 20+ students, struggles to maintain group safety and cohesion because she lacks a centralized tool to calculate required chaperone-to-student ratios and obtain a clear, accessibility-aware guide for movement, dining, and seating within the museum.

Self-Review & Reflection

I have evaluated my digital wireframes against the following criteria to ensure clarity, usability, and completeness:

  • Complete User Flow: The design provides a seamless journey from dashboard selection to the final downloadable output, ensuring a user can successfully complete their task.
  • UI Consistency: All screens maintain consistent navigation, button styles, and card-based hierarchy, reflecting the information architecture required for mobile usability.
  • Hierarchy & Critical Thinking: I have prioritized the information Tara needs most—such as ratio validation and accessibility paths—placing them at the top of the hierarchy to reduce cognitive load.
  • From Paper to Digital: The transition from paper wireframes to high-fidelity digital frames in Figma was focused on refining spatial relationships and ensuring that UI elements were clearly defined for mobile constraints.
